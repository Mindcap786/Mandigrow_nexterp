GST for Mandi Commission Agents — FAQ

Do commission agents in mandi need to file GST?

Yes. Commission agents whose turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (₹10 lakhs in special category states) must register for GST. MandiGrow auto-calculates GST on every commission sale and generates GSTR-1 ready data for filing, making compliance effortless.

What is Mandi Tax (Cess) and which states have it?

Mandi Tax or Mandi Cess is a local levy charged on agricultural produce sold through APMC markets. It is separate from GST. States like Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Maharashtra, Punjab, and Madhya Pradesh levy Mandi Tax. MandiGrow auto-calculates Mandi Cess based on the state your mandi operates in.

Can MandiGrow generate GSTR-1 data for mandi traders?

Yes. MandiGrow automatically organizes all your sales data into GSTR-1 format — B2B (buyer-wise), B2C, and export records. Export ready for your CA or upload directly to the GST portal. No manual data entry, no spreadsheets.

Is MandiGrow compliant with APMC regulations?

Yes. MandiGrow is designed for APMC-registered mandis and commission agents. It supports the mandatory documentation — gate entries, lot records, commission pattis, and trade statements — required by APMC authorities in most states.

Does MandiGrow work for both registered and composition scheme dealers?

Yes. MandiGrow supports both regular GST registration and the Composition Scheme. For composition dealers, it tracks turnover thresholds and generates the simplified CMP-08 quarterly return data. Switch between tax modes without losing any transaction history.

How does MandiGrow handle GST for different fruit and vegetable varieties?

Most fresh fruits and vegetables are exempt from GST under Chapter 7 of the HSN code. However, processed, branded, or packed produce may attract 5% or 12% GST. MandiGrow lets you configure HSN codes and GST rates per item — and auto-applies them on every invoice.
